Philosophy

This course aims to develop a quantitative understanding of chemical bonding. Aspects of covalent and non-covalent bonding will be covered. At the end, the students should be able to understand various approximations that go into various quantum chemistry methods, and be able to assess the use those methods in their routine research.

0.1 Positron, a fundamental anti-particle predicted by Dirac. It has a mass that is same as that of electron ($m_e$) but opposite charge ($+e$). Positrons are emitted by Flouride-18 and used in commercial imaging for patients. Positronium is an analogue to hydrogen atom where the proton is substituted by a positron. Choose a coordinate system of your choice and a) Write down the non-relativistic Hamiltonian for positronium, b) Sketch a qualitative distribution of positronic and electronic wavefunctions, and c) Will this system be stable? If so, derive a formula for its energy levels. (10 pts)
